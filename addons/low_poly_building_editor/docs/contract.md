## Current contract

- The building editor is a Godot editor plugin and must stay out of the runtime app shell.
- `BuildingEditor3D` is the scene-owned coordinator for a building assembly; it owns grid snapping, eight-way drawing constraints, default wall, floor, stairs, pillar, and roof settings, individual wall and enclosed rectangular room creation, floor/stairs/pillar/roof creation, wall lookup, collinear wall merge detection, intersecting-wall detection for editor commits, geometry-only intersecting-wall clip refresh, same-eave roof overlap clipping, and roof-to-wall underside clipping.
- Selecting authored `Wall3D`, `Floor3D`, `Stairs3D`, `Pillar3D`, `Roof3D`, or any `BuildingOpening3D` subtype in the editor updates the active building tool mode through the dock/toolbar path; the typed `Window3D` and `Door3D` branches determine the opening mode, with metadata retained only as a legacy fallback for base-class openings. Generic prop scene nodes do not auto-select Prop mode because they currently have no persistent prop marker.
- `Wall3D` stores zero or more typed `WallSegment3D` resources in its exported `segments` array. Segment zero, when present, supplies the node transform; every segment stores its own parent-local endpoints, height, thickness, and color. An empty array is valid and generates no mesh or collision. Hidden `start_point`, `end_point`, and `extra_segments` compatibility aliases load legacy scenes but are not inspector-facing authored state. Shared joint endpoint drags move every connected segment endpoint together, single endpoint drags can disconnect from or reconnect to a shared joint, zero-length endpoint drags can promote another segment to index zero when the old first segment is deleted, and one low-poly mesh/collision assembly rebuilds from authored segments, sibling clip spans, roof clip surfaces, and direct child openings.
- Wall meshes miter shared non-collinear endpoints by extending wall side faces to their endpoint-direction side-line intersections without adding butt-style joint caps, keep those miter points invariant when the same span is drawn in the opposite start/end direction, keep top and bottom caps tied to wall footprints with targeted overlap clipping so 3+ split joins render filled while enclosed wall loops keep their room interiors open, clip overlapping faces in plan space so crossings, T-junctions, and corners avoid square butt seams, open miter gaps, and z-fighting caps, and remove generated wall geometry above visible roof bottom surfaces where roofs intersect walls.
- The dock persists a Wall Type of `Wall` or `Room`. Wall mode draws one grid-snapped span and retains optional eight-way direction locking. Room mode treats the gesture points as opposite corners of an axis-aligned rectangle and creates one `Wall3D` node with four connected authored spans, one generated mesh/collision assembly, and one undo/redo action.
- When separate rooms or walls contain matching collinear overlapping spans, scene order always owns the overlap: the earlier wall keeps the shared mesh/collision and the later wall clips its duplicate portion, independently of `merge_intersecting`. Authored spans remain unchanged so moving either room restores the appropriate geometry.
- Door and window placement is allowed on either wall of a matching collinear shared room boundary, independently of scene order; the wall that renders the shared span cuts the opening (authored or propagated from the clipped sibling). Crossings, T-junctions, and different-thickness spans reject the opening.
- An opening node parented to any wall of a matching collinear overlap is cut from every collinear sibling that shares the span, in both scene-order directions: the coordinator maps the opening into each receiving wall's segment-local span. The scene-earlier wall renders the shared mesh and cuts the visible hole; because collision is generated per wall and is not sibling-clipped, each collinear wall also cuts the opening from its own collision so the duplicated overlap collision does not block the doorway. The mapping is transient rebuild data passed alongside the sibling clip segments, never edits authored spans, and never reparents the opening node; editing the opening refreshes the affected walls through the geometry-clip signature.
- Wall drag edits are segment-aware: Shift-clicking a wall body inserts a joint by splitting that span into two connected segments, dragging an isolated endpoint changes only the picked segment endpoint, dragging a shared joint endpoint changes every connected segment endpoint at that joint, Option/Alt-dragging a shared joint endpoint disconnects only the picked endpoint, dragging a single endpoint near another endpoint or joint snaps it there to connect, dragging a wall body translates the wall's authored segment set, zero-length endpoint drags delete the collapsed segment or wall node through undo/redo, drag commits normalize same-wall intersections, and crossings with other wall instances refresh sibling clip geometry without merging wall nodes.
- Dragging the body of one side of a four-span rectangular room resizes that room perpendicular to the selected side, moves both connected corner endpoints, leaves the opposite side fixed, preserves opening anchors, rejects a zero-width or zero-depth result, and commits through one undo/redo action. Option/Alt-dragging a rectangular room body bypasses side resizing and translates the full authored segment set instead, grid-snapped, preserving opening anchors, through one undo/redo action. Body dragging an irregular or open wall network continues to translate the full authored segment set.
- `Floor3D` stores opposite parent-local footprint corners in `start_point/end_point` plus local-space rectangular `floor_holes`; the node transform derives from the minimum X/Z top corner, thickness extends downward from the top surface, and one low-poly slab mesh plus generated concave collision rebuilds from those corners and any internal openings. Solid Floor tool edits move the body or resize edges/corners on the active floor grid, preserve the existing top-surface Y, reject zero-area slabs or resizes that would push stored holes outside the slab, and commit through undo/redo. Hole Floor tool commits append interior rectangular holes through undo/redo and merge intersecting holes into a non-overlapping rectangular union that preserves the combined outline.
- `Stairs3D` stores opposite parent-local footprint corners in `start_point/end_point`, derives its transform from the minimum X/Z lower-entry corner plus `stair_rotation_degrees`, and rebuilds one stepped low-poly mesh plus generated concave collision from total height, step count, underside thickness, rotation, and color. Generated side-wall box collision blockers sit inside the left and right footprint edge strips under the treads and are split per tread so their top edges follow the stair profile; `HumanBody3D` cannot enter the stairs from the side, but a character already on a step can leave or jump off sideways without hitting a full-height invisible rail or collision outside the stair footprint. Stairs tool creation converts the first draw point and current draw point into opposite corners of the rotated footprint. Stairs tool edits move the body, resize edges/corners on the active stairs grid in the stairs' rotated local frame, rotate around the footprint center, preserve the existing lower-entry Y, reject zero-area footprints, and commit through undo/redo.
- `Pillar3D` stores only universal pillar state: parent-local `base_point`, lower radius, optional upper-radius override, height, upper/lower rim settings, color, rebuild state, and collision behavior. `RoundPillar3D` and `TaperedPillar3D` inherit the internal variable-sided layer that owns `side_count`; `SquarePillar3D` and `OctagonalPillar3D` own their fixed geometry without exposing that unused control. Pillar tool edits move the body or resize radius on the active pillar grid, preserve the existing base Y, reject undersized radii, and commit through undo/redo.
- `Roof3D` stores only universal roof state: opposite parent-local footprint corners, thickness, overhang, rotation, color, covered render regions, debug settings, rebuild state, and collision behavior. `FlatRoof3D`, `ShedRoof3D`, `GableRoof3D`, and `HipRoof3D` identify the serialized style. The internal sloped-roof layer owns the face-angle property used by shed/gable/hip roofs; only `HipRoof3D` owns the hip gable drop. Each style delegates its generated height, surface sampling, top faces, topology, and edge splits to its matching geometry strategy. Roof tool creation instantiates the concrete style before deriving overhang, generated height, and top geometry. Simple hip roofs use a horizontal ridge along the longer footprint axis, two triangular short faces, two trapezoid long faces, and the same configured angle on all faces; square hip footprints degenerate to a centered apex. Positive hip gable drop clips the hip ridge ends downward from the peak and extends the ridge as needed to preserve the configured face angle, producing a half-hip or jerkinhead variant. Roof tool edits move the body, resize edges/corners on the active roof grid in the roof's rotated local frame, rotate around the footprint center, preserve the existing eave-plane Y, keep the roof angle fixed, reject zero-area and fully covered roofs, keep same-eave roofs as separate authored footprints while clipping only polygon render regions whose roof surface sits under another roof surface, close clipped edges with fascia faces, provide visible underside surfaces that clip intersecting wall mesh/collision, recompute sibling roof covers, and commit through undo/redo. When enabled, `debug_show_triangle_wireframe` creates a generated `RoofTriangleWireframe` line mesh from the final triangle index buffer.
- `BuildingOpening3D` is the shared persistent child-node base for window and door holes. Its `opening_width`, `opening_height`, segment-local position, frame properties, and collision settings define the common opening rectangle and casing contract consumed by the wall mesh compiler. Openings are mapped to the nearest wall segment and restored into that segment's updated frame when wall geometry changes (including endpoint, joint, extra-segment, and whole-wall drags).
- `Window3D` owns only behavior applicable to every window opening and generates no style geometry. The internal `window_pane_3d.gd` layer owns pane depth/color plus plain-pane primitives for glazed pane-bearing styles. Every concrete class owns its geometry entry point: single/double windows choose their pane layout, `GridWindow3D` builds its muntins, `LouveredWindow3D` builds its slats, `TransomWindow3D` builds its rail, `ArchedWindow3D` builds its stepped fillers, and `WindowFrame3D` intentionally builds no pane content.
- `Door3D` owns only behavior and defaults applicable to every door opening and generates no style geometry. The internal `door_leaf_3d.gd` layer owns panel depth/color plus plain-leaf primitives for styles with physical leaves. Every concrete class owns its geometry entry point: single/double doors choose their leaf layout, `GlazedDoor3D` builds its solid/glass split, `GlazedGridDoor3D` adds its lite muntins, `PanelDoor3D` builds its inset grid, `DutchDoor3D` builds its split leaves and mid rail, and frame styles intentionally build no leaf content.
- The editor must instantiate the concrete class for the selected style for both previews and committed nodes; style properties and style geometry remain in that class, not in conditionals on `BuildingOpening3D`, `Window3D`, or `Door3D`. Primary parts (panes, panels/leaves, slats) carry collision while decorative overlays do not, and style details never change the opening rectangle the wall compiler reads. `frame_sides`, `frame_protrusion`, and `wall_thickness` remain shared base properties and only change the casing depth.
- Legacy scenes that stored solid `door_panel_count`, `door_panel_depth`, or `door_panel_color` values directly on `BuildingOpening3D` continue to deserialize those values through storage-only compatibility properties. New authoring must use a `Door3D` subtype.
- New overlapping collinear walls of matching thickness and height merge by extending the retained wall endpoints instead of creating duplicate spans.
- Non-collinear intersecting walls sharing a base plane remain separate authored `Wall3D` nodes: sibling spans are supplied as non-rendered clip segments, each wall renders only its own authored spans, buried side and cap geometry is removed in plan space, same-height cap ownership follows scene order, roof underside cuts are supplied as transient non-rendered clip surfaces, and undo/redo refreshes clip geometry without reparenting children or removing other wall nodes.
- In editor mode, `BuildingEditor3D` watches authored wall and roof geometry signatures and refreshes transient clipping data when those source properties change outside the plugin commit path.
- Generated wall solid collision cells, floor/stairs/pillar/roof collision, floor-hole side geometry, and opening frame parts are rebuild artifacts identified by metadata; they are not author-facing source nodes.

## Building Block Style Pattern

Use this pattern for every future serialized building-block family that has distinct styles:

1. The family base class owns only properties and behavior applicable to every style. It may provide low-level mesh, material, collision, lifecycle, and transform primitives, but it must not export a style selector or branch on style to generate geometry.
2. Add an internal intermediate class only when a real subset shares authored properties or primitives. Examples are pane-bearing windows, leaf-bearing doors, variable-sided pillars, and sloped roofs. Styles outside that subset must not inherit unused inspector properties.
3. Every serialized style has a concrete class. That class owns style identity, style-only exported properties, defaults, and its geometry entry point. Large reusable shape math may live in a one-to-one style geometry strategy, but it must remain selected by the concrete class.
4. Editor factories map persisted dock style keys to concrete scripts. Both previews and committed nodes come from the same factory; changing a preview style recreates the preview with the correct class instead of mutating a style flag.
5. Compatibility APIs may route an old style key to the concrete strategy for legacy consumers, but routing and storage adapters must be isolated from the new-authoring object model. They must not reintroduce style properties or geometry branches into the family base.
6. Frame-only or empty styles intentionally override the geometry entry point with no inner content. Shared dimensions consumed by another compiler—such as an opening rectangle read by `Wall3D`—remain on the family base and cannot be changed by decorative style geometry.
7. Tests must verify inheritance, inspector-property ownership, factory class selection, each style's generated geometry, and compatibility behavior. A base-class property regression is a contract failure even when the generated mesh still looks correct.
8. A tool option that performs an operation is not automatically a serialized style. `Room` creates a four-segment `Wall3D`, and `Floor Hole` modifies a `Floor3D`; neither needs a `Room3D` or `FloorHole3D` subtype unless it becomes persistent polymorphic scene state.

Before adding a style switch to an existing block, first decide whether the distinction persists in the scene. Persistent polymorphism follows this hierarchy; one-shot editor operations remain coordinator/plugin workflows.

## Governance

- Keep building-editor scene mutations undoable through `EditorUndoRedoManager`; placed wall, room, floor, stairs, pillar, roof, prop, and opening mutations — including deletion of a zero-length wall segment or its final wall node — must go through it so undo/redo restores scene hierarchy and rebuilt meshes together.
- If wall segment storage, floor corner or floor-hole storage, stairs footprint storage, pillar base/radius storage, roof footprint storage, segment splitting, child reparenting, opening semantics, generated collision, merge behavior, or editor interaction changes, update this contract and [`feature.md`](feature.md).
- Do not treat `BuildingEditor3D` buildings as runtime story targets or save/resume anchors without updating the low-poly 3D prototype contract in [`../../../docs/contracts.md`](../../../docs/contracts.md) and the relevant gameplay docs.
