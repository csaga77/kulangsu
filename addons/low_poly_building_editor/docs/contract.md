## Current contract

- The building editor is a Godot editor plugin and must stay out of the runtime app shell.
- `BuildingEditor3D` is the scene-owned coordinator for a building assembly; it owns grid snapping, eight-way drawing constraints, default wall, floor, stairs, pillar, and roof settings, floor/stairs/pillar/roof/wall creation, wall lookup, collinear wall merge detection, intersecting-wall detection for editor commits, geometry-only intersecting-wall clip refresh, same-eave roof overlap clipping, and roof-to-wall underside clipping.
- Selecting authored `Wall3D`, `Floor3D`, `Stairs3D`, `Pillar3D`, `Roof3D`, or `BuildingOpening3D` nodes in the editor updates the active building tool mode through the dock/toolbar path; generic prop scene nodes do not auto-select Prop mode because they currently have no persistent prop marker.
- `Wall3D` stores parent-local `start_point` and `end_point` for its primary span plus typed `WallSegment3D` resources in `extra_segments` for authored extra spans and manually inserted joints; transient sibling clip segments and roof underside surfaces are used only for generated mesh/collision clipping and do not merge wall instances or edit wall spans. The node transform derives from the primary span, shared joint endpoint drags move every connected segment endpoint together, single endpoint drags can disconnect from or reconnect to a shared joint, zero-length endpoint drags can promote an extra span to primary when the old primary is deleted, one low-poly mesh rebuilds from authored spans, sibling clip spans, roof clip surfaces, and direct child openings, and opening-aware solid box collision cells rebuild from authored spans, direct child openings, and roof clip surfaces so `HumanBody3D` cannot pass through wall faces.
- Wall meshes miter shared non-collinear endpoints by extending wall side faces to their endpoint-direction side-line intersections without adding butt-style joint caps, keep those miter points invariant when the same span is drawn in the opposite start/end direction, keep top and bottom caps tied to wall footprints with targeted overlap clipping so 3+ split joins render filled while enclosed wall loops keep their room interiors open, clip overlapping faces in plan space so crossings, T-junctions, and corners avoid square butt seams, open miter gaps, and z-fighting caps, and remove generated wall geometry above visible roof bottom surfaces where roofs intersect walls.
- Wall drag edits are segment-aware: Shift-clicking a wall body inserts a joint by splitting that span into two connected segments, dragging an isolated endpoint changes only the picked segment endpoint, dragging a shared joint endpoint changes every connected segment endpoint at that joint, Option/Alt-dragging a shared joint endpoint disconnects only the picked endpoint, dragging a single endpoint near another endpoint or joint snaps it there to connect, dragging a wall body translates the wall's authored segment set, zero-length endpoint drags delete the collapsed segment or wall node through undo/redo, drag commits normalize same-wall intersections, and crossings with other wall instances refresh sibling clip geometry without merging wall nodes.
- `Floor3D` stores opposite parent-local footprint corners in `start_point/end_point` plus local-space rectangular `floor_holes`; the node transform derives from the minimum X/Z top corner, thickness extends downward from the top surface, and one low-poly slab mesh plus generated concave collision rebuilds from those corners and any internal openings. Solid Floor tool edits move the body or resize edges/corners on the active floor grid, preserve the existing top-surface Y, reject zero-area slabs or resizes that would push stored holes outside the slab, and commit through undo/redo. Hole Floor tool commits append interior rectangular holes through undo/redo and merge intersecting holes into a non-overlapping rectangular union that preserves the combined outline.
- `Stairs3D` stores opposite parent-local footprint corners in `start_point/end_point`, derives its transform from the minimum X/Z lower-entry corner plus `stair_rotation_degrees`, and rebuilds one stepped low-poly mesh plus generated concave collision from total height, step count, underside thickness, rotation, and color. Generated side-wall box collision blockers sit inside the left and right footprint edge strips under the treads and are split per tread so their top edges follow the stair profile; `HumanBody3D` cannot enter the stairs from the side, but a character already on a step can leave or jump off sideways without hitting a full-height invisible rail or collision outside the stair footprint. Stairs tool creation converts the first draw point and current draw point into opposite corners of the rotated footprint. Stairs tool edits move the body, resize edges/corners on the active stairs grid in the stairs' rotated local frame, rotate around the footprint center, preserve the existing lower-entry Y, reject zero-area footprints, and commit through undo/redo.
- `Pillar3D` stores a parent-local `base_point` plus lower radius, optional upper radius override, height, side count, style, upper/lower rim height and outset, and color; the node transform derives from the base point, and one styled low-poly pillar mesh plus generated collision rebuilds from those properties. Pillar tool edits move the body or resize radius on the active pillar grid, preserve the existing base Y, reject undersized radii, and commit through undo/redo.
- `Roof3D` stores opposite parent-local footprint corners in `start_point/end_point`, derives its transform from the minimum X/Z eave corner plus `roof_rotation_degrees`, and rebuilds one styled low-poly roof mesh plus generated collision from style, roof face angle in degrees, thickness, overhang, rotation, color, covered render-area bounds or simplified cover polygons, hip gable drop, and optional debug triangle wireframe settings. Roof tool creation converts the first draw point and current draw point into opposite corners of the rotated base footprint before deriving overhang, generated height, and style-specific top geometry. Simple hip roofs use a horizontal ridge along the longer footprint axis, two triangular short faces, two trapezoid long faces, and the same configured angle on all faces; square hip footprints degenerate to a centered apex. Positive hip gable drop clips the hip ridge ends downward from the peak and extends the ridge as needed to preserve the configured face angle, producing a half-hip or jerkinhead variant without changing roof style. Roof tool edits move the body, resize edges/corners on the active roof grid in the roof's rotated local frame, rotate around the footprint center, preserve the existing eave-plane Y, keep the roof angle fixed so generated height is derived from the current style-specific roof run, reject zero-area and fully covered roofs, keep same-eave roofs as separate authored footprints while clipping only polygon render regions whose roof surface sits under another roof surface, include matching overhangs in clipping, keep face-level intersection edges straight instead of stair-stepped through tiny rectangle fragments, close clipped edges with fascia faces so roof thickness remains visible along cuts, provide visible underside surfaces that clip intersecting wall mesh/collision above the roof bottom, recompute sibling roof covers, and commit through undo/redo. When enabled, `debug_show_triangle_wireframe` creates a generated `RoofTriangleWireframe` line mesh from the final triangle index buffer.
- `BuildingOpening3D` is the persistent child node used for window and door holes; its `opening_width`, `opening_height`, and segment-local position define the opening rectangle consumed by the wall mesh compiler, mapped to the nearest wall segment and restored into that segment's updated frame when wall geometry changes (including endpoint, joint, extra-segment, and whole-wall drags). Window variants may add one or two generated pane meshes, and door variants may omit the generated bottom frame and add one or two generated panel meshes, while still using the same opening rectangle contract.
- New overlapping collinear walls of matching thickness and height merge by extending the retained wall endpoints instead of creating duplicate spans.
- Non-collinear intersecting walls sharing a base plane remain separate authored `Wall3D` nodes: sibling spans are supplied as non-rendered clip segments, each wall renders only its own authored spans, buried side and cap geometry is removed in plan space, same-height cap ownership follows scene order, roof underside cuts are supplied as transient non-rendered clip surfaces, and undo/redo refreshes clip geometry without reparenting children or removing other wall nodes.
- In editor mode, `BuildingEditor3D` watches authored wall and roof geometry signatures and refreshes transient clipping data when those source properties change outside the plugin commit path.
- Generated wall solid collision cells, floor/stairs/pillar/roof collision, floor-hole side geometry, and opening frame parts are rebuild artifacts identified by metadata; they are not author-facing source nodes.

## Governance

- Keep building-editor scene mutations undoable through `EditorUndoRedoManager`; placed wall, floor, stairs, pillar, roof, prop, and opening mutations — including deletion of a zero-length wall segment or its final wall node — must go through it so undo/redo restores scene hierarchy and rebuilt meshes together.
- If wall endpoint storage, floor corner or floor-hole storage, stairs footprint storage, pillar base/radius storage, roof footprint storage, segment splitting, child reparenting, opening semantics, generated collision, merge behavior, or editor interaction changes, update this contract and [`feature.md`](feature.md).
- Do not treat `BuildingEditor3D` buildings as runtime story targets or save/resume anchors without updating the low-poly 3D prototype contract in [`../../../docs/contracts.md`](../../../docs/contracts.md) and the relevant gameplay docs.
